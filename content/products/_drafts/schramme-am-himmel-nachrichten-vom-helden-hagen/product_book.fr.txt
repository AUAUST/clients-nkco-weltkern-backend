  <p>For several years, Jonathan Meese and Alexander Kluge were preoccupied with Hagen von Tronje, the fabled character from The Song of the Nibelungs. They were both mesmerized by this mysterious figure, whom Jonathan Meese renamed ”Hagen von Troja“ (Hagen of Troy) one day. The poetic excavations of the Hagen principle stretch from antiquity to Silicon Valley and from Middle High German folktales to Jan Philipp Reemtsma’s book Warum Hagen Jung­ Ortlieb erschlug: Unzeitgemäßes über Krieg und Tod by way of Fritz Lang’s film recordings.<br />
